Q1: What is a barcode? A1: A barcode is a machine-readable optical label that contains information about the item to which it is attached. Barcodes are used to track and identify products in retail stores, warehouses, and other industries.

Q2: What are the types of barcodes? A2: The two main types of barcodes are 1D (linear) and 2D (matrix) barcodes. 1D barcodes are the most common and contain a series of black and white bars. 2D barcodes are more complex and contain patterns of small squares, dots, and other shapes.

Q3: How do I scan a barcode on an Android phone? A3: You can scan a barcode on an Android phone by downloading a barcode scanning app. After installing the app, you can point your phone’s camera at the barcode and the app will read the barcode and display the information it contains.

Q4: What type of information is stored in a barcode? A4: Barcodes can contain a variety of information such as product names, SKUs, prices, and manufacturer information.

Q5: Are there any other uses for barcodes? A5: Yes, barcodes can also be used in other industries such as healthcare, transportation, and logistics to track and identify items.
