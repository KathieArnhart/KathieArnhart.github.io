1. Explaining How to Check AirPods Battery With the System Menu#

One of the most convenient features of AirPods is the ability to check the battery level without needing to reach for your phone. It’s easy to use the system menu to check your AirPods battery on Android without an app. Here’s how.

First, you’ll need to open the quick settings menu, which can be done by swiping down from the top of your phone’s home screen. From there, long-press on the Bluetooth icon. This will bring up a list of all your connected Bluetooth devices.

Select your AirPods from the list. On the next screen, you’ll be able to check the battery percentage of both the left and right AirPods. You can also tap the three dots at the top right corner to access options to rename your AirPods or unpair them from your device.

If you can’t find the Bluetooth icon in the quick settings menu, you can access it from the settings menu. Just tap on the settings menu app and select Connected Devices from the list. From there, you’ll be able to follow the same steps as above.

Finally, you could also check your AirPods battery level directly from your phone’s notification area. Swipe down from the top of your home screen to access the notification area. If your AirPods are connected to your phone, the battery level of each individual AirPod will be displayed at the bottom of the notification area.
